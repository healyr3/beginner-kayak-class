River 4 Overview

For your graduation paddle we will be transitioning from a class environment to attending club trips and running your own personal river trips. We want you to be familiar with the process of signing up for a club trip, but also expose you to the resources available when choosing and organizing your own river trip!

Read Let's Go Boating about HOW to register for river trips.

Pre-Trip Organization

  • Safety equipment before leaving home (float bags, spare paddle, first aid kit, etc.)
  • Planning where to meet: The "put-in" is upstream & the "take-out" is downstream
  • Shuttle vehicles

Transitioning from Class to Club/Personal Trips

  • Get out & paddle right after class (with each other too!)
  • Set goals
  • Work on making hard moves on easier runs
  • Always work on your roll!
  • Take a class: Rescue, CPR, Wilderness First Aid, etc.
  • Use “us” as a resource

Safety Talk

  • Communication (paddle, whistle, hands – pass ALL signals back!)
  • Hazards
  • Self-rescue and getting assistance
  • River running safety (lead, sweep, spacing, group awareness, let someone know if following, rescue people first-gear second, stay in the eddy if not comfortable rescuing)
  • Logistics
