  • The Three Golden Rules

    • Separate your body movements. Allow your upper & lower body to work independently.

    • Use the power of your torso. The large muscles of your torso maximize the strength of your stroke.

    • Maintain control with an active blade. Get your next stroke in the water as soon as you take one out.

Gear Care

  • Hang your drysuit and other gear up when you get home. If your gear is not properly dried for storage, it will develop the 'river funk' smell.

  • If washing your drysuit, use a mild non-detergent soap and either hand wash or use a delicate cycle. It is good practice to clean your drysuit every once in a while to rinse off dirt that wears the suit over time. Do not use cleaning materials such as bleach as they will melt the rubber and ruin the suit.

  • Applying seal saver (303 protectant) from time to time will increase the lifespan of your neck and wrist gaskets.

  • If the drysuit zippers are difficult to operate or stuck, applying a zipper wax will improve usability.
